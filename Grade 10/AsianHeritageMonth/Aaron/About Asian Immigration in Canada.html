About Asian Immigration in Canada

Asian Immigration to Canada Timeline
January 01, 1885Chinese workers were brought in to help construct the railway, but immigration was restricted by the $50 head tax that was later increased to $500.
January 01, 1897More than 5,000 South Asians, over 90% of them Sikhs, came to British Columbia before their immigration was banned in 1908.
January 01, 1902 About 2,000 Doukhobors from Russia landed in Halifax en route to farms in the West. Refugees from Russia, especially Jews, Mennonites, and Doukhobors, settled in Canada.
January 01, 1903The federal government concluded that the Asians were “unfit for full citizenship … obnoxious to a free community and dangerous to the state” (Canadian Encyclopedia, 2016, para. 5).
January 20, 1904The head tax for Chinese immigrants was increased to $500.
October 01, 1906The ban on Chinese immigration was disallowed.
January 01, 1907The Japanese vessel Suian Maru landed at Beecher Bay on Vancouver Island. The group of 80 men and three women subsequently settled on Don and Lion islands near Richmond, BC.
September 07, 1907An order-in-council banned immigration from India and South Asian countries.
January 01, 1923Several hundred people rioted through Vancouver’s Asian district to protest Asian immigration to Canada.
